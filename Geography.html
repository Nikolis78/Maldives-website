The Geography

Geography

The Maldives is an island nation in the Indian Ocean, southwest of Sri Lanka and India. It consists of 1,192 coral islands,
which are grouped into 26 natural atolls. These islands stretch over approximately 90,000 square kilometers (34,750 square miles), making the Maldives one of the world's most geographically dispersed countries.

Key Geographic Features:

Atolls and Coral Reefs: The Maldives is formed from coral reefs, which have built up over time on the rims of ancient submerged volcanoes.
These reefs protect the islands from erosion and provide a rich habitat for marine life.

Climate

The Maldives enjoys a warm, tropical monsoon climate, with two main seasons: Dry Season (November - April): Less rainfall, calm seas, and sunny weather. Wet Season (May - October): More rainfall, stronger winds, and occasional storms.

The Maldives: A Paradise at Risk from Rising Sea Levels"

Because of its low elevation, the Maldives is highly vulnerable to rising sea levels caused by climate change.
This has led to global discussions on environmental conservation and sustainable tourism in the region.
