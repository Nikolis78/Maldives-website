The Culture

The Maldivian culture is a rich blend of South Asian, African, Arab, and Southeast Asian influences, shaped by centuries of trade and migration.
It is deeply rooted in Islam, the country's official religion, and reflects a unique maritime heritage

1.Language

The official language is Dhivehi, an Indo-Aryan language with influences from Arabic, Sinhalese, and Hindi.
English is widely spoken, especially in tourism and business.

2.Religion and Traditions

Islam is the state religion, and all Maldivian citizens are required to be Muslim by law.
Daily life is influenced by Islamic customs, including prayers, fasting during Ramadan, and religious festivals such as Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-Adha.

3.Clothing

Traditional attire for men is the Sarong (Mundu) with a long-sleeved shirt.
Women wear a Dhivehi Libaas, a long, colorful dress often adorned with embroidery.
In modern times, Western-style clothing is common, but modest dressing is encouraged due to Islamic traditions.

4.Festivals and Celebrations

Independence Day (July 26) – Celebrated with parades and cultural performances.
National Day (based on the Islamic calendar) – Marks the victory against Portuguese occupation.
Ramadan & Eid Celebrations – Special prayers, feasts, and communal gatherings take place.
